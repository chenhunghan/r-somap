findBMU

The selection of BMU is based on a batch comparision of similarities between the sample vector and the all map units \( m_{i} \). The similarity is defined as the smallest Euclidean distance from \( x(t) \). The BMU or the \( m_{c} \) is defined as

\[ \|x(t)-m_{c}\| = \min{\|x(t)-m_{i}\|} \]

There are two types of finding algorithms. The findBMUinRect returns the ( [x, y] ) on a rectangle map. The findBMUinHex returns the ( [x, y] ) on a hexagonal map. The default is the findBMUinHex.

stepwiseRecursiveSOM

The original, classical or stepwise recursive SOM algorithm.

At time \( t \), one sample vector \( x(t) \) from input data set is selected. By comparing the selected sample vector \( x(t) \) and all map units \( m_{i} \) on the map. One map unit \( m_{c} \) will be the "winner", or the best matching unit(BMU). The selection of BMU is eleborated in section findBMU function.

After the BMU ( m{c} ) has been identified, all ( m{i} ) will be modified. The modification depends on the mathematical form of the neighborhood function ( h_{ci}(t) ). Which is eleborated in section h, the neighborhood function. The modification is

\[ m_{i}(t+1) = m_{i}(t) + h_{ci}(t)[x(t) - m_{i}(t)] \]

The rates of the modifications \( x(t) - m_{i}(t) \) at different nodes \( m_{i} \) depend on the \( h_{ci}(t) \). Where \( c \) is the best matching unit on map and \( i \) is the index of node in the map.

The step wise recursive SOM will reiterated the modifications for given epochs.

\[ \sum_t{m_{i}(t+1)} = \sum_t{m_{i}(t)} + \sum_t{h_{ci}(t)[x(t) - m_{i}(t)]} \]

alpha

The \( \alpha(t) \) returns the learning rate at time \( t \). The \( \alpha(t) \) is a decreasing function of time which returns values between \( [0,1] \) (decreases from 1 to 0). \( \alpha(t) \) should be monotonically (e.g., hyperbolically, exponentially, or piecewise linearly) decrease function of \( t \). (Kohonen, 2012)

In r_somap, for stepwise recursive version of SOM, the linearly function

\[ \alpha(t) = rate_{0} - t / T \]

is used. Where \( rate_{0} \) is the initial rate, and \( T \) is the total numbers of epochs. \( rate_{0} \) has a default value of 0.5 since this was suggested by SOM Toolbox

sigma

The \( \sigma(t) \) function returns the neighborhood radius at time \( t \), an decreasing function of t. The \( \sigma(t) \) should be monotonically (e.g., hyperbolically, exponentially, or piecewise linearly). (Kohonen, 2012)

In r_somap, the default is a linear function

\[ \sigma(t) = (radius_{0} - radius_{T}) * (T - t) / T \]

returns series of values from given initial radius to given final radius. Where \( radius_{0} \) is the initial radius, and \( radius_{T} \) is the final radius. \( (T - t) / T \) is the fraction of \( t \) in \( T \) ( the total numbers of epochs).

The final radius has a default that is \( \frac{\sqrt{1}}{2} \) since the "map spacing" or the padding of nodes in the hexagonal map in r_somap is \( \sqrt{1} \). The final radius should be pre-defined since "The final value of \( \sigma(t) \) shall not go to zero, because otherwise the process loses its ordering power. It should always remain, say, above half of the map spacing."(Kohonen, 2012)

h

The \( h_{ci}(t) \), or the neighborhood function. The rates of the modifications \( x(t) - m_{i}(t) \) at different nodes \( m_{i} \) depend on the mathematical form of the \( h_{ci}(t) \). Where \( c \) is the index of best matching node on map and \( i \) is the index of node in the map. \[ m_{i}(t+1) = m_{i}(t) + h_{ci}(t)[x(t) - m_{i}(t)] \]

In r_somap, the neighborhood function h(t) is

\[ h_{ci}(t) = \alpha(t)\exp [-dist^{2}(c,i)/2\sigma^{2}(t)] \]

which is the same as much applied choice. (Kohonen, 2012)

The \( \alpha(t) \) is the learning rate function and \( \sigma(t) \) is the neighborhood radius function. \( dist^{2}(c,i) \) is the square of the geometric distance between the best matching node \( c \) and \( i \) is the index of node in the map.
